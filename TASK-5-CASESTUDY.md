Title: UPI Fraud Case Study

With the increasing use of digital payments in India, UPI fraud has become a common cybercrime. In a typical case, a fraudster contacts a victim pretending to be a bank representative or a customer. They send a payment request or a fake link and ask the user to approve it, claiming that money will be credited to their account.

Many users, unaware of how UPI works, enter their PIN or approve the request. Instead of receiving money, the amount gets deducted from their account. In some cases, fraudsters also create fake apps or websites that look similar to official banking platforms to steal login details.

This type of fraud mainly occurs due to a lack of awareness and careless sharing of sensitive information. Victims often realize the fraud only after the money has been transferred, making recovery difficult.

To prevent such incidents, users must understand that UPI PIN should never be shared and is only required when sending money, not receiving it. Being cautious and aware can help reduce such cybercrimes significantly.
